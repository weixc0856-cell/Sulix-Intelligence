## BLUF

AI agents are rapidly becoming capable of executing longer, more complex tasks across platforms, which will fundamentally reshape how startups build products and automate workflows.

## Analysis

**Impact:** Founders should prioritize integrating AI agent capabilities into their product roadmaps within the next quarter to avoid being disrupted by competitors who leverage these advancements.

**Geopolitical:** OpenAI published research demonstrating AI agents performing extended, complex work tasks, while Google DeepMind released Gemini 3.5 Flash with built-in computer use, allowing agents to interact across platforms. These developments signal a major leap in AI agent autonomy and cross-system operability.

**Supply Chain:** For startups, this means reduced reliance on human-in-the-loop for multi-step processes, lower cost of automating workflows, and new opportunities to build agent-driven products. Industries like SaaS, customer support, data processing, and software development will see rapid automation of previously manual tasks.

This confirms the thesis that AI agents are moving from narrow, single-task tools to general-purpose autonomous workers. The simultaneous release by OpenAI and Google indicates a race, making it urgent for founders to adopt these capabilities. No change in the direction, but the pace has accelerated.
