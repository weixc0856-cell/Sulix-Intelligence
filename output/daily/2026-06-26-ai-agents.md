## BLUF

AI agents are entering enterprise production at scale, requiring founders to prioritize agent-native infrastructure and integration strategies within 6 months.

## Analysis

**Impact:** Founders should evaluate their product for embedding AI agents as a feature or building agent middleware to capture value from the enterprise wave.

**Geopolitical:** OpenAI published research showing AI agents enable longer, complex tasks; Google DeepMind released Gemini 3.5 Flash with built-in computer use for agents; OpenAI expanded enterprise deployments like Samsung rolling out ChatGPT Enterprise and Codex globally; Cloudflare introduced temporary accounts for AI agents. These events indicate a coordinated push by major players to operationalize AI agents across software stacks.

**Supply Chain:** The availability of agent-capable models (Gemini, GPT-4o/Codex) and deployment infrastructure (Cloudflare ephemeral accounts) lowers the barrier for startups to build agent-powered features. However, incumbents like OpenAI and Google are capturing platform value, so founders must differentiate on specific use cases or vertical integrations rather than generic agent capabilities.

The thesis that AI agents are transitioning from demos to production is strongly confirmed. Previously, agent workflows were fragile and limited to research labs. Now, concrete enterprise deployments (Samsung) and standardized infrastructure (Cloudflare temporary accounts) validate that the bottleneck is shifting from model capability to deployment and integration. This challenges the assumption that agents are years away from practical business impact.
