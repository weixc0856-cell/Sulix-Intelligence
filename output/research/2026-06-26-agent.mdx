## Executive Summary

Recent advances in agent capabilities by OpenAI and Google signal a leap toward autonomous AI assistants, increasing enterprise automation potential while raising regulatory and geopolitical concerns. Organizations must act quickly to harness these agents for competitive advantage but also prepare for workforce disruption and new data governance challenges. Proactive engagement with standards, retraining, and risk mitigation is essential.

## Geopolitical Assessment

Countries may accelerate AI regulation to manage autonomous agents, while tech leaders race to set standards. Geopolitical tensions could rise over AI sovereignty and data control.

## Technical Impact

Enterprises will automate more workflows, reducing labor costs but raising retraining needs. Competitors without advanced agents risk obsolescence, sparking consolidation and new service models.

## Commercial Framework

Enterprises that quickly integrate advanced agents can reduce labor costs and gain efficiency, but must navigate a fragmented regulatory landscape and potential backlash. Incumbents without agent capabilities risk obsolescence, while new service models (agent-as-a-service) may emerge. Strategic investments now will determine competitive positioning in an agent-driven economy.

## Risk Scenarios

- Base (60%): Steady adoption: agents improve productivity but face moderate regulation, leading to gradual workforce shifts and competitive dynamics
- Adverse (25%): Regulatory backlash: governments impose strict controls on autonomous agents, limiting deployment and increasing compliance costs
- Aggressive (15%): Breakthrough adoption: agents achieve full autonomy, rapidly displacing jobs and creating new market leaders; geopolitical tensions spike over AI control
