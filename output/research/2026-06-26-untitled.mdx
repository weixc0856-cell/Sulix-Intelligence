## Executive Summary

Two major hardware innovations—IBM's sub-1nm transistor and OpenAI/Broadcom's LLM-optimized inference chip—threaten Nvidia's GPU dominance and accelerate US tech decoupling with China. The race for customized AI semiconductors intensifies, pressuring foundries and driving government investments. Companies must adapt by piloting custom chips and diversifying supply chains to maintain competitive edge.

## Geopolitical Assessment

The US and its allies could strengthen their lead in AI hardware, intensifying export controls and technology decoupling with China. This may trigger a renewed race for semiconductor dominance, with government investments and alliances focused on advanced fabrication and specialized chips.

## Technical Impact

Nvidia's GPU dominance faces a potential threat as custom inference chips like OpenAI/Broadcom's could offer lower cost and higher efficiency for AI workloads. IBM's sub-1nm transistor pushes the limits of Moore's Law, pressuring foundries like TSMC and Samsung to accelerate their roadmaps. Cloud providers and AI startups stand to benefit from cheaper, faster inference.

## Commercial Framework

The semiconductor landscape is shifting from GPU-centric to specialized AI chips, creating winners among custom chip designers and cloud providers while threatening incumbents. Companies must pivot from hardware procurement to strategic co-development, and investors should prioritize differentiation in architecture over pure scale.

## Risk Scenarios

- Base (60%): Custom chips gradually erode Nvidia's market share over 2-3 years
- Adverse (25%): Export controls escalate, disrupting supply chains for advanced chips
- Aggressive (15%): Sub-1nm breakthrough enables unexpected performance leap, rendering current designs obsolete
