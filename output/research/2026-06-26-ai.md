## Executive Summary

Recent AI capability leaps (GPT-5, Gemini) intensify US-China tech rivalry and may trigger new export controls and governance frameworks. Industries reliant on complex cognitive tasks face rapid automation and cost reduction, while tech giants gain dominance. To stay competitive, firms must invest in proprietary AI capabilities and retrain workforces, while policymakers should accelerate governance frameworks and strategic investments.

## Geopolitical Assessment

The leap in AI capabilities intensifies US-China tech rivalry, with both nations accelerating investments to secure strategic advantages. It may trigger new export controls on AI chips and models, and prompt international governance frameworks to manage risks.

## Technical Impact

Industries reliant on complex cognitive tasks (e.g., pharma, legal, software) face rapid automation, lowering costs but displacing mid-level professionals. Tech giants with advanced models gain dominant positions, while startups struggle to keep pace without massive compute resources.

## Commercial Framework

The commercial landscape shifts towards winner-take-most dynamics in AI, where owning both compute and data infrastructure is key. Firms must balance between building proprietary AI and leveraging commoditized models, while preparing for potential geopolitical disruptions to supply chains. The strategic imperative is to secure access to cutting-edge AI while upskilling the workforce to keep pace with automation.

## Risk Scenarios

- Base (60%): Continued rapid AI advancement with measured regulatory response; leading tech firms capture majority of value.
- Adverse (25%): New export controls disrupt access to advanced chips and models; AI development slows and costs rise.
- Aggressive (15%): AI capabilities surpass expectations, enabling full automation of many knowledge-worker tasks; massive job displacement and geopolitical tensions escalate.
