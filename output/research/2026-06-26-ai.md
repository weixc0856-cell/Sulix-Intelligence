## Executive Summary

Rapid advances in AI agent capabilities by OpenAI and Google DeepMind are enabling autonomous task execution, which will drive productivity gains and automation. This creates competitive advantages for early adopters and geopolitical stakes for leading nations, while regulatory divergence risks market fragmentation. Action is required to capture value, manage disruption, and navigate strategic risks.

## Geopolitical Assessment

Countries leading in AI agent development gain economic and strategic advantages, potentially widening power imbalances and sparking a new wave of tech-driven geopolitical competition. Regulatory divergence across regions could lead to trade tensions and a fragmented global AI market.

## Technical Impact

Industries like customer service, software development, and logistics see rapid productivity gains as AI agents automate complex tasks. Early adopters gain competitive advantages, while laggards face disruption; new business models emerge around agent-as-a-service.

## Commercial Framework

The shift to AI agents will create significant first-mover advantages, especially in customer-facing and operational processes. Companies that integrate agents into their core workflows can reduce costs and improve service, but must manage ethical and regulatory risks. New revenue streams from agent-as-a-service models will emerge, threatening traditional software and service providers.

## Risk Scenarios

- Base (60%): AI agents gradually adopted across industries, with productivity gains of 10-20% in target sectors within 2 years.
- Adverse (25%): Rapid adoption leads to job displacement and public backlash, triggering restrictive regulations that slow deployment.
- Aggressive (15%): Agent capabilities leapfrog expectations, enabling full automation of knowledge work, causing extreme disruption and winner-takes-all dynamics.
