## Executive Summary

OpenAI and DeepMind are advancing AI agents, intensifying global competition and disrupting knowledge work industries. Early adopters gain productivity advantages, but regulatory and safety concerns arise. Actions must balance rapid adoption with risk management.

## Geopolitical Assessment

The advancement of AI agents by US-based OpenAI and Google DeepMind could intensify global competition, prompting other nations to accelerate their own AI agent development for economic and strategic advantage. This may lead to new international regulations focused on safety and control of autonomous systems.

## Technical Impact

The ability of AI agents to perform longer, complex tasks will disrupt industries reliant on knowledge work, such as software development, customer service, and data analysis. Companies that adopt these agents early could gain significant productivity advantages, while laggards may struggle to compete.

## Commercial Framework

The AI agent race offers first-mover advantages but carries regulatory and ethical risks. Companies must balance rapid deployment with safety and compliance to sustain long-term competitive edge. Strategic partnerships with AI leaders could mitigate R&D costs.

## Risk Scenarios

- Base (60%): Gradual adoption of AI agents across industries, with incremental productivity gains and moderate regulatory frameworks.
- Adverse (25%): Regulatory backlash or safety incidents slow adoption, causing investment losses and public distrust.
- Aggressive (15%): Rapid, unregulated adoption leads to mass job displacement and competitive disruption, but also huge productivity leaps for leaders.
