## Executive Summary

OpenAI and Broadcom have introduced Jalapeño, a custom AI chip that threatens NVIDIA's GPU dominance by improving inference efficiency and reducing costs. This innovation could reshape AI hardware supply chains and accelerate LLM adoption, prompting competitors to respond. Actions should focus on reassessing hardware strategies, pursuing diversification, and investing in custom chip capabilities.

## Geopolitical Assessment

This custom chip could reduce global reliance on NVIDIA's GPUs, potentially shifting AI hardware supply chains. It may also prompt other nations to invest in domestic AI chip development to maintain competitiveness.

## Technical Impact

Jalapeño disrupts the AI chip market dominated by NVIDIA and Google TPUs, forcing competitors to accelerate custom chip efforts. It also lowers inference costs, enabling broader LLM adoption and new applications.

## Commercial Framework

The Jalapeño chip disrupts the existing AI hardware paradigm by offering a dedicated inference solution that could commoditize GPUs, lowering barriers for new entrants and expanding the total addressable market for AI applications. Companies must re-evaluate their vertical integration strategies and consider that long-term competitive advantage may come from ownership of custom silicon rather than exclusive access to NVIDIA's ecosystem.

## Risk Scenarios

- Base (60%): Jalapeño achieves moderate adoption, reducing inference costs by 30-40% and gaining 10-15% market share within 12 months
- Adverse (25%): Production delays or performance bottlenecks limit Jalapeño's impact, maintaining NVIDIA's dominance
- Aggressive (15%): Jalapeño proves superior, triggering rapid adoption and 30%+ cost reduction, leading to a surge in LLM applications and global race for custom chips
