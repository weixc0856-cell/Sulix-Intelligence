## Executive Summary

Situation: Major AI labs are launching autonomous agents capable of long-running tasks. Complication: This triggers international competition and regulatory gaps, while enterprises face both opportunities and risks. Resolution: Nations and enterprises should proactively establish governance frameworks and invest in agent safety to capture benefits while mitigating harms.

## Geopolitical Assessment

Nations may compete to lead in autonomous agent technology, potentially escalating AI arms races. Regulatory frameworks will struggle to keep pace, raising concerns about cross-border accountability for agent actions and data sovereignty.

## Technical Impact

Enterprises can automate complex workflows, boosting efficiency but disrupting business models reliant on human labor. Competition will intensify among AI labs to offer the most capable and trustworthy agents, with early movers capturing significant market share.

## Commercial Framework

Enterprises that integrate agents early will gain significant efficiency, but must prioritize trust and control to avoid liability. AI labs compete on safety and reliability, where market leaders will capture network effects. Regulators face a choice between fostering innovation and preventing harm, with cross-border coordination essential to avoid fragmentation.

## Risk Scenarios

- Base (60%): Gradual enterprise adoption with moderate incidents, leading to measured regulation over 2 years
- Adverse (25%): A high-profile agent failure (e.g., unauthorized financial transaction) triggers public backlash and swift, restrictive regulation
- Aggressive (15%): Rapid deployment of agents by leading labs leads to economic disruption and a technology arms race among nations
