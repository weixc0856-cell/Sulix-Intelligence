## Executive Summary

Recent announcements from IBM (sub-1nm process) and OpenAI (custom AI chip) signal accelerating specialization in semiconductors, intensifying US-China tech competition. This shift threatens traditional fabless-foundry models and may trigger tighter export controls. Immediate action is needed to reassess supply chains and investment in custom AI hardware.

## Geopolitical Assessment

The push for sub-1nm and custom AI chips intensifies US-China tech competition, as advanced semiconductor capabilities become critical for AI leadership. Export controls on chipmaking tools and designs may tighten, affecting global supply chains.

## Technical Impact

Specialization disrupts the traditional semiconductor model: fabless companies like OpenAI design custom chips, while foundries like TSMC benefit from advanced nodes. Intel's position may weaken if it fails to compete in both logic and foundry.

## Commercial Framework

The semiconductor industry is bifurcating into general-purpose and AI-specific chips, with profound implications for fabless companies, foundries, and end-users. Companies must decide whether to build custom AI hardware or leverage generic GPUs, balancing performance, cost, and supply chain security. The US-China dimension adds geopolitical risk that could shift competitive advantages.

## Risk Scenarios

- Base (60%): Gradual specialization: custom AI chips become mainstream, but general-purpose chips remain important. US-China competition leads to moderate export restrictions, impacting only leading-edge nodes.
- Adverse (25%): Escalating trade war: strict export controls on chipmaking equipment and design tools, disrupting global supply chains. AI chip access becomes restricted, slowing AI development.
- Aggressive (15%): Rapid breakthrough: sub-1nm process becomes viable sooner than expected, giving first-mover advantage to IBM and partners. Custom AI chips achieve order-of-magnitude performance gains, disrupting incumbents.
