## Executive Summary

New AI agent capabilities from OpenAI and Google DeepMind enable autonomous task execution, intensifying US-China tech rivalry and regulatory challenges. Companies must act now to integrate agents for productivity gains while managing geopolitical and accountability risks.

## Geopolitical Assessment

Increased AI autonomy may intensify US-China tech rivalry as nations compete for leadership in AI agents, potentially leading to export controls on agentic AI technologies. Regulatory frameworks may struggle to keep pace with autonomous decision-making, raising concerns about accountability and control.

## Technical Impact

Companies across sectors like customer service, software development, and data analysis can automate complex workflows, reducing operational costs. This intensifies competition as early adopters gain productivity advantages, pressuring laggards to invest or partner with AI providers.

## Commercial Framework

Early adopters of AI agents can achieve 20-30% cost reduction in knowledge work, but must navigate geopolitical fragmentation and evolving regulations. Companies that delay risk competitive obsolescence, while those investing now gain strategic leverage through automation and data advantages.

## Risk Scenarios

- Base (60%): Gradual adoption of AI agents with moderate regulatory alignment, productivity gains of 10-20% for early movers
- Adverse (25%): Stringent new export controls on agentic AI or regulatory restrictions on autonomous decisions
- Aggressive (15%): Rapid commoditization of AI agents leads to winner-take-most dynamics; laggards face existential disruption
